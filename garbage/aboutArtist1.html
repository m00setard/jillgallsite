About Jill Coulsell

Discover the story behind Jill's art and journey.

Biography

Jill has been painting from the early 1980’s and has since won many awards in that time. During that time‚ the Herald Sun Outdoor Art Show which was held annually‚ saw many of her Melbourne Tram paintings going overseas‚ especially to the U.K., U.S.A., and China.

Likewise her seascapes and many of her Lighthouse scenes have won her many awards‚ and have been sought by galleries. Jill enjoys her painting‚ and prefers to create movement in her pictures giving them the feeling of being on the spot. One could almost feel the sea spray!

Jill has a wide interest in painting pictures that challenge her‚ and her versatility in any subjects proves itself.

Education

Chris Canning: 8 Weeks in 1978 with Chris Canning drawing and sketching still life in the studio and on location in the Dandenong Ranges.
John Spink: 1980’s Approximately 5 years Oil painting of landscapes and seascapes.
Alan Martin: 1990’s Summer workshop at various locations.

During the 1990’s Jill developed her own painting styles in her home studio.
Kevin Boucher: 2001 Joined Kevin's Art group. This has involved the use of acrylics and oils‚ further development of landscape/seascape painting and techniques for studio and location painting. Jill is still a member of Kevin's very interesting art group today.

Michael Goff: Jill has had classes and workshops with Michael in the studio and on location including Thornton‚ Monsalvat, and Mordialloc Creek‚ which have been a valuable experience in her learning. Jill is currently a member of the Sherbrooke Art Society. This society runs monthly meetings involving a demonstration by different artists each month‚ they also run workshops and have an impressive gallery.
